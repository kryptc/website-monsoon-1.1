when, with elation
you will greet yourself arriving
at your own door, in your own mirror
and each will smile at the other's welcome,
and say, sit here. Eat.
You will love again the stranger who was your self.
Give wine. Give bread. Give back your heart
to itself, to the stranger who has loved you

HAMILTON - An American Musical



Hamilton is a Broadway musical that scored a record breaking 16 Tony nominations, about the life of American Founding Father Alexander Hamilton, written by Lin-Manuel Miranda, a modern-day Mozart. It is a revolutionary piece of theatre, mixing hip-hop, rap, R&B and Broadway flawlessly. While at first glance the show seems to revolve around men who lived centuries ago, Lin manages to include inclusivity, diversity and female empowerment through his masterful lyrics. Even the casting is colour-blind with straight white characters being played by actors from all races and backgrounds. The casting, as Miranda has said, is more reflective of America now and is meant to display the country’s diversity. Part of the show’s relatablility is in the audience seeing themselves in it.

The source material for the musical may be over 200 years old, but somehow, the show pulls out themes that are still relevant today. Every character is relatable and are represented as real people with a lot of grey areas in them. The musical itself is a mashup of music, genre and people in a way that is reflective of our own society. Hamilton is a universal and timeless story that Miranda has presented in the most impactful way.

the language of the mountains is rain


A coming-of-age story that encompasses pretty much every genre you can think of into one seamless tale spun around Eiji Miyake, a young Japanese man, searching for a father he's never met. He travels to a post-modern Tokyo, where he begins his search amidst a city of millions, all the while trying to reconcile with the memories of his dead sister. In my opinion, the author, David Mitchell is given far less credit than he deserves for this masterpiece in fiction. Mitchell has mastered a certain style of writing where authors use many different styles of prose and use vocabulary specific to each time and setting. In number9dream, he transitions between fantastical daydreams to Yakuza standoffs to World War 2 journals, weaving a different yet connected thread in each part. For the first time, I felt more attached to the atmosphere created by the strange writing than the actual protagonist, partly because of a long-standing attraction towards Japanese themes and because I'm a sucker for a book that doesn't really fit into any genre but captures your imagination nonetheless.

TARANTINO


"Violence is what makes movies good."

Presenting Quentin Tarantino, film maker extraordinaire, with a penchant for excessive fake blood splurting onto his lens. What's not to love?
1. Utterly captivating dialogues/monologues, that keep it casual but interesting
2. Non-linear plots
3. Unapologetically violent scenes
4. Range varies from the Wild West to a dystopian World War 2 setting
5. Gripping soundtrack and scene progression

With movies like Inglourious Basterds, Pulp Fiction and Django Unchained tacked to his name, it's almost inspirational how he manages to make each film as fresh and original as his previous ones.

GIRLS/GIRLS/BOYS


Panic! at the Disco is an American rock band from Las Vegas, Nevada, formed in 2004 and featuring vocalist Brendon Urie, bassist Dallon Weekes, guitarist Kenneth Harris and drummer Dan Pawlovich. The band recorded and released its debut studio album, A Fever You Can't Sweat Out in 2005. Popularized by the second single, "I Write Sins Not Tragedies", the album was certified double platinum in the US. Interestingly, Panic! at the Disco have been known to change their sound each album.
